# Bail & Custody Guidance

## Description
Provides step-by-step guidance on applying for regular bail or anticipatory bail, and understanding police arrest procedures.

## Trigger Keywords & Triggers
The skill triggers when the query contains words like: bail, anticipatory bail, arrest warrant, police arrest, custody, bailable, non-bailable, police lockup.

### Positive Examples
  - "How to file for anticipatory bail for Section 498A?"
  - "My brother is arrested in an assault case, how to get bail?"
  - "What is the difference between bailable and non-bailable offences?"

### Negative Examples
  - "Makan malik deposit nahi de raha."
  - "Consumer complaint registration."

## Workflow Steps
1. Identify the FIR details and the specific sections applied by the police.
2. Classify offences as Bailable (bail is a right at the police station) or Non-Bailable (discretion of court).
3. If arrest is feared: File an Anticipatory Bail application in Sessions Court or High Court under Sec 482 BNSS / 438 CrPC.
4. If already arrested: File a Regular Bail application under Sec 480 BNSS / 437 CrPC (Magistrate) or Sec 483 BNSS / 439 CrPC (Sessions).
5. Arrange matching surety bonds and local sureties as requested by the court.

## Risk Detection Logic
Check for: Flight risk, tampering with witnesses, severity of charges, and delay in seeking anticipatory bail.

## Output Format
Provide: Arrest rights checklist (DK Basu), bail options analysis, surety bond requirements, and draft anticipatory bail petition.

## References Structure
- Section 480 BNSS (Section 437 CrPC) - Regular Bail
- Section 482 BNSS (Section 438 CrPC) - Anticipatory Bail
- D.K. Basu guidelines on Arrest

## Assets Structure
- Anticipatory Bail application template
- Surety Bond undertaking format
